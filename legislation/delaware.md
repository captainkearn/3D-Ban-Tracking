# Delaware

Status: passed-active

## Enacted Law

- House Bill 125, enacted in the 151st General Assembly, created Delaware's untraceable firearm and 3D-printing firearm provisions.
- 11 Del. C. Section 1463 makes it a felony for an unlicensed person to use a 3D printer or similar device to manufacture or produce a firearm, firearm receiver, or major firearm component.
- It also covers distributing digital instructions to unlicensed persons when the instructions may be used to program a 3D printer to manufacture a firearm, receiver, or major component.

## Active Proposal

- HB 399, introduced June 11, 2026, would require 3D printers sold or delivered in Delaware to include firearm-blocking technology after feasibility review and implementing regulations.
- The bill would create a working group to recommend performance standards and determine whether the blocking-technology requirement is technologically and operationally feasible.
