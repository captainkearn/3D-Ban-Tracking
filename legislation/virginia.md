# Virginia

Status: current-law

Last checked: 2026-07-16.

## Summary

- HB 40 was enacted in 2026 and creates new restrictions on unfinished frames or receivers and unserialized firearms.
- The introduced bill text defines "manufacture or assemble" to include additive, subtractive, or other processes.
- It defines "unfinished frame or receiver" to include a forging, casting, printing, extrusion, machined body, or similar item that may readily be completed, assembled, or converted to function as a frame or receiver.
- Giffords describes the enacted law as prohibiting importation, purchase, sale, transfer, manufacture, assembly, and later possession of unserialized firearms / frames / receivers, with most provisions taking effect January 1, 2027 and the possession provision taking effect July 1, 2027.

## Notes

This is not a printer-sale blocking mandate. It is a ghost-gun / unserialized-firearm law that reaches additive manufacturing and printed unfinished frames or receivers.
